Protect Yourself From Phishing Attacks

Cybercriminals use phishing to steal passwords, banking details, and personal information. Learn how to stay protected.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers pretend to be trusted organizations to trick users into revealing confidential information.

How to Recognize Phishing Emails

  • Suspicious sender email addresses
  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Unexpected attachments or links
  • Grammar and spelling mistakes
  • Requests for passwords or confidential information

How to Detect Fake Websites

  • Misspelled URLs
  • No HTTPS security lock
  • Poor website design
  • Fake login pages
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers

Social Engineering Tactics

Attackers manipulate people using psychological tricks.

  • Creating urgency or fear
  • Pretending to be banks or managers
  • Emotional manipulation
  • Impersonating trusted companies

Best Practices to Stay Safe

Verify Senders

Check email addresses carefully before responding.

Avoid Suspicious Links

Never click unknown or shortened URLs.

Enable MFA

Use Multi-Factor Authentication for extra protection.

Keep Software Updated

Update systems regularly to fix vulnerabilities.
